About Solarpunk Guide

Our Mission To build the most helpful, honest, and easy-to-read guide resource for the Solarpunk community — no fluff, no clickbait, just useful information written by people who actually play the game.

Why We Built This

Solarpunk is an ambitious survival crafting game set in a world where technology and nature coexist in harmony. With floating islands, airships, solar-powered machines, and a deep crafting system, there's a lot to learn. We noticed that many game guide websites are either stuffed with intrusive ads, written by people who've never touched the game, or abandoned after a few months. We wanted something different.

This site exists because we genuinely love the genre. We love the slow mornings spent chopping wood and the late nights designing automated power grids. We love the first time you get your airship airborne and the first time a creeper destroys your carefully built greenhouse. Wait — wrong game. But you get the idea.

Who We Are

Solarpunk Guide is run by a small team of survival game enthusiasts. We've collectively sunk thousands of hours into games like Minecraft, Valheim, Satisfactory, Raft, and Astroneer. When the Solarpunk demo dropped, we knew this was going to be something special.

Our writers have been playing since the early demos and betas. Every guide on this site is based on firsthand gameplay — we don't rewrite press releases or summarize other people's YouTube videos. If we recommend a strategy, it's because we've tested it ourselves.

What You'll Find Here

As the game grows, so will this site. Here's what we're building:

  • Beginner guides — First-day survival, basic crafting, early exploration
  • Crafting & building guides — Recipe breakdowns, automation setups, base design tips
  • Exploration guides — Island biome breakdowns, resource locations, hidden secrets
  • Technology trees — Research paths, machine upgrades, solar grid optimization
  • Farming & sustainability — Crop cycles, animal care, eco-friendly power systems
  • Airships & vehicles — Building, upgrading, and piloting your flying machines

How We Keep the Lights On

This site costs money to run — domain renewal, the occasional coffee to fuel late-night writing sessions — but we refuse to plaster it with pop-ups, autoplay videos, or intrusive ads. We display a small number of respectful banner ads through our advertising partner. That's it. No paywalls, no "sign up to read more," no nonsense.

If you find our guides helpful, the best way to support us is simply to keep reading and share them with other players. Thank you for being here.
