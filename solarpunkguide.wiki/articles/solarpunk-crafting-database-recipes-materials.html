Welcome to the complete Solarpunk crafting database! Whether you're a fresh castaway on your starter island or an experienced engineer building a fully automated base, this guide catalogs every essential crafting recipe in the game — organized into four clean categories: 🔨 Basic Tools, 📦 Processed Materials, 🍞 Food & Consumables, and ⚙️ Mechanical & Automation Parts. Bookmark this page — you'll be coming back often.

🖱️ Quick Tip: Press F to open the quick-build menu anywhere. For advanced recipes, interact with the appropriate workstation (Workbench, Furnace, Advanced Fabricator). In co-op mode, once any player unlocks a recipe, the entire team gains access — no need to research it twice!

🔨 Section 1: Basic Tools — Hand-Crafted, No Workbench Required

These are your day-one essentials. All stone-tier tools are crafted directly from the quick-build menu (press F) using only raw materials found on the ground. Iron-tier upgrades require an Anvil to unlock the recipe, then an Iron Workbench to craft.

💡 Progression Tip: Rush iron tools as soon as you build a Furnace. The 60% efficiency boost effectively triples your productivity across an entire play session. Mine 20 iron ore (red-speckled rocks, common on mountain islands), smelt into ingots, and never look back at stone tools.

📦 Section 2: Processed Materials — Universal Crafting Components

These semi-finished materials are the backbone of every recipe in the game. You'll need them for buildings, furniture, storage, research, and advanced machinery. Process them at the appropriate workstation.

⚠️ Cotton Is Gold: Never waste early-game cotton on decorative items. Prioritize cloth for the Research Table, then for rope (needed for storage chests and animal pens). Cotton is farmed from crops, not gathered in the wild — every seed matters.

💡 Bonus Tips & Pro Strategies

👥 Co-op Synergy: In multiplayer, recipe unlocks are team-wide. Have one player rush the Research Table while another gathers raw materials — you'll cut early-game grind by nearly half. Any recipe one player researches is instantly available at every team workstation.

♻️ Don't Trash Your Junk: Excess tools, spoiled food, and obsolete gear can all be fed into the Recycler Machine. You'll earn eco-points redeemable for exclusive seeds, building skins, and decorative items that are otherwise unobtainable. It's the game's sustainability mechanic in action — nothing goes to waste.

⚡ Power Dependency Alert: Irrigation Cores, Drone Modules, and Recycler Cores all draw power continuously. Before crafting any automation component, make sure your energy grid can handle the additional load. The formula is simple: Total Daily Power Generation ≥ Sum of All Device Power Draws. A blackout stops everything.
