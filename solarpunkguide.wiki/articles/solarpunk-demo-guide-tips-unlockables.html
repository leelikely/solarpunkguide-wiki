Solarpunk Demo Guide: 10 Essential Tips & All Unlockable Content

May 30, 20268 min read
In This Guide

The free Solarpunk demo dropped on Steam on February 24, 2026 during Steam Next Fest — and it has already been downloaded over 500,000 times. While your demo save won't carry over to the full release on June 8, it's the perfect sandbox to learn every core mechanic before launch day. Here's everything you need to know.

📋 Demo at a Glance ~2–3 hours of playtime · Starter island (~1 km²) + one neighboring island · Unlocks up to Tier 2 tech · Single-player only · No full airship (prototype only)

🧠 10 Must-Know Beginner Tips

1. 🍓 Prioritize Food and Water Immediately

Forget chopping trees — your first action should be gathering at least 20 berries from nearby bushes. Berries restore both hunger and thirst, making them the ultimate early-game survival food. With a stockpile, you'll have enough buffer time to build proper water collectors and farms.

2. 📍 Choose Your Base Location Wisely

Look for the central flat plain on the starter island — it's close to water, near multiple resource nodes, and has ample space for expansion. Avoid cliff edges (dangerous to build on) and overly dense forests (block sunlight for solar panels).

3. 🔬 Build the Research Table Before Anything Else

After crafting a basic Workbench, your next priority is the Research Table. It unlocks every advanced structure in the game. Rush to research the Rainwater Collector, Bed, and Solar Panel — in that order. These three items form your survival foundation.

4. ⚡ Set Up Energy Early — Don't Wait Until Nightfall

Build at least 1 Solar Panel + 1 Battery on your very first day. Place the panel on high ground for maximum efficiency. Without power, your workbench and lights go dark at night — and navigating in pitch blackness is no fun.

5. 🌿 Cotton Is Your Most Precious Early Resource

Cotton crafts into Cloth, which is required for the Research Table, advanced furniture, and airship components. Don't waste your first cotton on decorative clothing — funnel it all into research and essential infrastructure.

6. ⚡ Use the Quick-Build Menu (F Key)

Press F to open the radial quick-build menu. It contains every structure you've unlocked. Press R to rotate buildings before placing, and Q to cycle through style variants. Mastering quick-build cuts your construction time in half.

7. 🌲 Don't Clear-Cut Your Forest

Leave saplings and small trees standing — they regenerate over time. If you chop down every single tree on your island, you'll have no sustainable wood supply and will be forced to travel farther and farther for basic materials.

8. 🌧️ Exploit the Weather System

When it rains, all farmland is auto-watered — this is the perfect time for mass planting. During thunderstorms, wind turbines produce double power. Plan your activities around the weather forecast for maximum efficiency.

9. 📦 Build Storage Chests and Stay Organized

Don't dump items on the ground. Craft several Storage Chests and sort items by type: wood/stone in one, food/seeds in another, crafted materials in a third. An organized base saves you hours of searching later.

10. 🔍 Explore Every Corner of Your Starter Island

The starter island hides hidden chests, rare resource nodes, and blueprint fragments along its edges and in small caves. Take 20 minutes to walk the entire perimeter — you'll find materials that give you a significant early advantage.

🎯 What to Practice Before Launch

Spend 1–2 hours in the demo focusing on: basic movement and resource gathering → base site selection and foundation building → energy system setup (solar + battery) → farming and animal basics → Research Table tech unlock order. When the full game drops on June 8, you'll hit the ground running while others are still learning the controls.
