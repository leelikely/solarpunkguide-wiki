Solarpunk Mid-Game Guide: From Basic Survival to a Fully Automated Empire

June 23, 20269 min read
In This Guide
  1. Loading contents…

You've got a roof over your head, iron tools in your hands, and a steady supply of food and water. Congratulations — you've survived the early game. Now comes the real question: what's next? Many players stall out here, repeating the same daily grind of chopping trees and smelting ore without a clear direction. This guide gives you that direction.

🎯 Mid-game definition: You've met three criteria: ① Stable food & water (hunger/thirst consistently above 70%) ② Iron tools unlocked (60% faster gathering) ③ Basic energy grid operational (solar + wind, no nighttime blackouts). If you haven't met all three, solidify your early game first — rushing into mid-game with a shaky foundation leads to resource collapse.

🎯 The 5 Mid-Game Goals (in Priority Order)

The mid-game's core philosophy: automate the repetitive, explore the unknown, upgrade everything. Here's the exact order:

#GoalWhy It's Priority
1⚡ Energy System UpgradeEvery automation device needs power. Upgrade energy first, or everything else stalls.
2🌾 Farm AutomationFood is the foundation. Automate it and you free up hours of daily manual labor.
3⛏️ Mining AutomationIron and stone are your most-used materials. Stop swinging a pickaxe manually.
4🛩️ Begin ExplorationYour starter island can't provide rare resources. Build an airship and expand your world.
5💱 Establish TradeConvert surplus production into rare items through merchant platforms.

⚡ Priority 1: Energy System Upgrade

Why first: Every automation device draws power. An undersized grid means constant brownouts and stalled production.

Must-do:

  • 💨 Unlock Wind Power → build 2–3 new Wind Turbines on high ground. This fills the nighttime and cloudy-day gap that pure solar leaves.
  • 🔋 Double your battery bank (4–6 total). More storage = more resilience through consecutive cloudy days.
  • 💧 Optional: If your island has a waterfall, build a Water Generator for rock-steady baseload power.

Target: 24/7 uninterrupted power. Every device should run whenever you need it, not "when the sun's out."

🔌 Pro move: Install manual power switches on your production zone. Furnaces and fabricators draw standby power even when idle — flipping them off when not in use reclaims ~20% of your total daily output.

🌾 Priority 2: Farm Automation

Why second: Manual farming eats 30–40% of your daily playtime. Automate it and you reclaim those hours for exploration and building.

Must-do:

  • 💧 Unlock Auto-Irrigation → place one Irrigator per 3×3 farmland grid. Every plot should be covered.
  • 📐 Expand to 20–30 farmland plots. Plant ratio: 3 Wheat : 2 Cotton : 1 Sunflower. Wheat feeds you. Cotton feeds industry. Sunflower feeds animals and produces oil.
  • 🐔 Unlock Basic Animal Care → Chicken Coop + Feeding Trough + Water Trough. Steady eggs and feathers with zero daily effort.

Target: Food and agricultural materials are fully self-sustaining. You never think about hunger. Cotton and wheat stockpile automatically.

⛏️ Priority 3: Mining Automation

Why third: Iron ingots and stone are your most-used mid-game materials. Hand-mining is the biggest time sink in Solarpunk. Automation here is a genuine progression leap.

Must-do:

  • ⛏️ Unlock Auto-Mining → place 2–3 Auto-Miners directly on iron and stone nodes.
  • 🔥 Expand to 2–3 Furnaces — raw ore from auto-miners flows straight into smelting. Stockpile fuel nearby.
  • 📦 Build a dedicated ore & ingot storage zone — labeled chests, no mixing with other materials.

Target: Stone and iron ore accumulate passively. You check storage chests and they're full. You haven't swung a pickaxe in hours.

🛩️ Priority 4: Begin Exploration

Why fourth: Your starter island has zero crystals, zero gold, zero diamonds. Rare resources only exist on distant floating islands. You need an airship to reach them.

Must-do:

  • 🛩️ Unlock Airship Technology → collect 10 Airship Components → build your first Solar Airship.
  • 🗺️ First flight route: Starter Island → Small Forest Island → Desert Island (mine sand for glass) → Mountain Island (mine rare ores).
  • 📍 Mark every island's coordinates on your map. Future you will thank present you when planning resource runs.

Target: Glass unlocked. Crystal and gold locations identified. A second island scouted for potential expansion.

💱 Priority 5: Establish Trade

Why fifth: Once you have surplus production (excess wheat, wool, truffles), trade converts that surplus into rare seeds, airship upgrade parts, and blueprints you can't get any other way.

Must-do:

  • 🏪 Find and repair a Merchant Platform (usually on medium/large islands with ruins).
  • 💰 Sell: Surplus wheat, wool, wood — renewable, high-volume resources.
  • 🛒 Buy: Rare crop seeds · Airship upgrade components · Advanced technology blueprints.

Target: A positive feedback loop: production → trade → rare upgrades → better production. The mid-game engine is now self-sustaining.

🔬 Optimal Mid-Game Tech Unlock Order

Follow this sequence exactly. Deviating means wasted research points and stalled progression:

  1. 💨 Wind Power
  2. 💧 Auto-Irrigation
  3. 🐔 Basic Animal Care
  4. ⛏️ Auto-Mining
  5. 🪓 Iron Tool Upgrades
  6. 🛩️ Airship Technology
  7. 🚁 Transport Drones
  8. 🏪 Merchant Trading

You can sprinkle in furniture or decoration techs between these as you like, but never delay a core tech for a cosmetic one.

🏗️ Mid-Game Base Restructuring

Don't tear down your starter base. Evolve it in place using the spine-extension method:

  • 🏭 Move production (furnaces, workbench, miners) to the downwind side — away from living quarters. Less noise, less visual clutter.
  • 🌾 Consolidate farmland into one connected zone for unified irrigation management.
  • 🏠 Relocate your living space to the scenic edge — upgrade to a multi-level home with balconies and a garden.
  • 🛩️ Reserve a flat edge zone for the airship dock and storage hub.
  • 🧱 Central spine corridor with storage at the center; production, farming, and living wings branching off. Add new wings as you grow.

🆘 Common Mid-Game Sticking Points

🔴 "I never have enough iron."
Build auto-miners on iron nodes immediately. Then fly to a Mountain island — iron ore density there is 3× higher than your starter island. Stop using iron for decorations.
🔴 "My power grid keeps failing."
Add wind turbines before more solar panels. Wind runs 24/7 and is more valuable per material cost. Turn off idle devices with switches. Expand batteries — storage is as important as generation.
🔴 "I feel stuck in a repetitive loop."
Set one small goal per session: "Today I build the airship." "This week I automate the farm." Goals create forward momentum. Also: explore a new island. New content breaks the grind.
🔴 "My base looks chaotic and I hate it."
Pause expansion. Spend one session purely on organization: group similar buildings, demolish abandoned temporary structures, clear debris, label storage. A clean base feels like a new game. Beautification comes later.

🚀 The mid-game payoff: When your auto-miners hum, your fields water themselves, and your airship is docked and ready — that's the moment Solarpunk transforms from a survival grind into a creative sandbox. Everything after this point is joy, not labor.
