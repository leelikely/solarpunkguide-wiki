Cyberwave Interview: How a Two-Person Team Built Solarpunk's Million-Wishlist Hit

May 30, 20268 min read
In This Guide
  1. Loading contents…

In an industry where triple-A games are routinely built by teams of hundreds over half a decade, here's a sobering fact: Solarpunk — a game with over 1 million Steam wishlists — was made by exactly two people. No publishers. No investors. Just two developers in Germany who quit their jobs in 2022, drained their savings, and spent four years building a floating-island paradise. We sat down with Ben Brass (artist) and Patrick de Rijk (programmer) for an exclusive look behind the scenes.

👥 The Team Ben Brass — former intern at Mimimi Games (Desperados 3). Patrick de Rijk — former programmer at Astragon Entertainment (Bus Simulator). Together: Cyberwave.

🤝 How They Met & Why They Started

Ben and Patrick met at a game developer meetup in Munich. "We both loved games that let players relax and be creative," Ben recalls. "But every survival game on the market was stressful — combat, monsters, constant pressure. We asked ourselves: what if survival could be cozy?"

In 2022, they both quit their jobs. "It was terrifying," Patrick admits. "We put all our savings on the line. But we believed in what we were making."

💰 The Kickstarter That Changed Everything

In summer 2023, Cyberwave launched a Kickstarter with a modest $30,000 goal. The campaign closed at over $330,000 — more than ten times the target. "That was the moment we realized we weren't alone," says Ben. "Thousands of people wanted the exact same game we wanted."

The funding gave them runway, but also pressure. "Suddenly there were tens of thousands of backers expecting a great game. We couldn't let them down."

💪 Four Years of 12-Hour Days

For the next two years, both developers worked 12–14 hours a day, six days a week. "We barely took breaks," Patrick says. "But I've never regretted a single day. Seeing players' faces when they first build an airship — that makes it all worth it."

February 2026: the demo launches during Steam Next Fest. 500,000 downloads in one week. Wishlists surge past the million mark. "We just stared at the numbers. We couldn't believe it."

🎯 Design Philosophy: No Combat, By Choice

The biggest question they get: Why no combat?

"Real life has enough stress and anxiety," Ben explains. "We didn't want to add more. In Solarpunk, your only enemy is your own imagination. Can you build something more beautiful? More efficient? More alive?"

Patrick adds: "Survival doesn't have to mean fighting monsters. It can mean building a home, growing food, and taking care of your environment."

The other pillar: environmentalism as gameplay. "Solarpunk isn't just an aesthetic — it's a lifestyle. We wanted players to feel the rhythm of the sun, to understand that taking care of nature takes care of you."

🧗 The Hardest Parts of Solo Development

  • ⏰ Time — "Two people handling programming, art, design, sound, marketing… every day is a triage."
  • ⚖️ Depth vs. accessibility — "We spent months tweaking the difficulty curve so newcomers feel welcome but veterans stay engaged."
  • 🎯 Expectations — "As wishlists climbed, so did pressure. That's why we skipped Early Access — we wanted to ship a complete game on day one."

🔮 What's Next After Launch?

Cyberwave has committed to at least one year of free content updates: new floating islands, new crops and animals, more automation features, and quality-of-life improvements based on player feedback.

"We want Solarpunk to be a place players can always come back to," Ben says. "A home in the sky."

Patrick's final message: "To everyone who wishlisted, backed us on Kickstarter, or just told a friend about the game — thank you. We built this for you. See you in the skies on June 8th."
