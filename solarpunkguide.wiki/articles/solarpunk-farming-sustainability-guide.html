Solarpunk Farming Guide: All Crops, Animals, Automation & Eco Systems

May 30, 20269 min read
In This Guide

Farming in Solarpunk isn't just about filling hunger bars — it's the backbone of your entire industrial supply chain. Cotton becomes cloth becomes airship components. Sunflowers become oil become advanced fuel. And the animal system, with its unique symbiotic mechanics, turns livestock from exploited resources into genuine partners. Here's the complete guide.

🔑 Crop Tips

  • 🌧️ Rain auto-waters all farmland — time mass-planting to rainfall.
  • ☀️ Full sunlight = maximum yield. Don't plant in building shadows.
  • 🏠 Greenhouse (glass walls) enables year-round rare crop growing, immune to weather.
  • 📐 Grid your plots: 10×10 with auto-irrigators between every 3 rows. Clean, efficient, scalable.

♻️ Sustainability: Zero-Waste Eco Loop

  • ♻️ Recycler: Convert surplus crops, food scraps, and unwanted items into recycle points.
  • 💩 Animal manure → compost → organic fertilizer — boosts crop yield without chemicals.
  • 💧 Rainwater collectors → irrigation → runoff captured — closed water loop.
  • 🌲 Leave saplings standing — trees regrow. Cut large, leave small. Perpetual wood supply.
  • ⚡ 100% renewable energy — solar, wind, water only. Zero fossil fuel, zero pollution.

🌍 The solarpunk philosophy in practice: Your farm should produce more than it consumes. Every waste product should feed back into the system. Animals aren't exploited — they're partners. This isn't just efficient gameplay — it's the message.

🚫 Farming Mistakes to Avoid

  • 🍓 Only planting berries — fine for hour 1, but you need wheat (bread) and cotton (industry) by hour 2.
  • 🧶 Neglecting cotton — it's the gateway to cloth, which gates the Research Table, advanced furniture, and airship components.
  • 💧 Manual watering large farms — unlock auto-irrigators before expanding beyond 10 plots. Your time is more valuable.
  • 🐄 Cramped animal enclosures — stressed animals stop producing and breeding. Give them space.
  • 🗑️ Throwing away food scraps — everything goes into the Recycler or compost pile. Zero. Waste.
