Solarpunk Food Guide: Hunger, Cooking Recipes, and Early Survival Tips

Hunger is just as important as thirst in Solarpunk. This guide explains where to find food, how cooking works, and how to keep yourself fed in the early game.

How Hunger Works

Your hunger meter slowly drops over time. If it gets too low, your character becomes weaker and work becomes much harder.

  • 🍽️ Above 50: normal performance
  • ⚠️ Below 20: lower stamina and slower work
  • 🩸 At zero: severe risk and collapse

Best Early Food Sources

The easiest food in the early game usually comes from foraging. Later, farming and cooking become the best long-term solutions.

  • 🍓 Berries — easy early food
  • 🌻 Sunflower seeds — quick snack and seed source
  • 🌾 Wheat — key ingredient for bread
  • 🥚 Eggs — useful for quick meals
  • 🐄 Milk — useful for more advanced recipes

Cooking Makes a Big Difference

Cooked food restores more hunger and often gives useful buffs. Once you unlock a cooking station, try to rely on meals instead of raw ingredients whenever possible.

How to Stay Fed Early On

For the first hour or two, focus on berries and simple meals. As soon as you can farm wheat and build a cooking station, your hunger situation becomes much easier to manage.

Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Relying only on raw food
  • ❌ Ignoring wheat and other cooking ingredients
  • ❌ Forgetting to bring food when exploring
  • ❌ Not building storage for ingredients

Final Tips

A steady food supply keeps your early game smooth. Once you have a few crops, a cooking station, and a small storage system, hunger becomes much easier to control.
