Solarpunk Demo Guide: 10 Essential Tips and All Unlockable Content

The Solarpunk demo is now free on Steam, giving players a great chance to learn the core systems before the full release. Here are 10 essential beginner tips plus a complete overview of everything you can unlock in the demo.

Why the Demo Matters

Even though demo saves will not carry over to the full game, the demo still gives you a real feel for the core gameplay loop. It is the best place to practice before launch day.

10 Essential Beginner Tips

1. Fix hunger and thirst first

Start by gathering berries as soon as you spawn. They are the easiest early food source and help solve both hunger and thirst at the same time.

2. Build near water on flat ground

A flat, open area close to water is the safest and most practical early base location. It makes building and resource transport much easier.

3. Make the research table early

Research is the key to unlocking everything important in the demo. Build the research table as soon as you can after creating your first workbench.

4. Prepare your energy system early

Don’t wait until night to think about power. A solar panel and a battery early on will keep your base functional after sunset.

5. Treat cotton as a valuable resource

Cotton is one of the most useful early materials. Use it carefully, especially when it comes to research and progression.

6. Learn the quick build menu

The quick build menu saves time and helps you place structures faster. Learn the rotate and style-switch shortcuts too.

7. Don’t clear every tree

Leave some saplings and young trees alone. Sustainable resource use is part of the game’s identity.

8. Use weather to your advantage

Rain can water crops for you, and stormy weather can change wind power output. Learn how weather affects your setup.

9. Use storage to stay organized

A few storage boxes will keep your inventory clean and make your base far easier to manage.

10. Explore the edges of the island

The demo map has hidden points of interest, chests, and useful resources. Exploring carefully pays off.

Final Advice

If you have one or two hours before launch, use the demo to practice the basics and build confidence. That way, when the full game arrives, you can jump in and start building right away.
